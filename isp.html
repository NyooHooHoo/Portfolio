Fever Fighters

Fever Fighters is a single-player survival video game where players learn to survive a cold. The game is targeted towards children from ages 5-6, as children aged 6 and younger are the most vulnerable to the common cold, getting an average of 6 to 8 colds per year (Stanford Medicine, n.d.; Worrall, 2011). Although many children at this age will be completely taken care of by their parents during a cold, around 1 in 5 parents have difficulty finding someone to stay home with their sick child due to work and financial reasons (C.S. Mott Children’s Hospital, 2017). As such, teaching this knowledge early is still beneficial to the well-being of young children.

The game was developed by BRRR Studios, a company consisting of project leader Lauren Hewang and project member Aidan Wang. All code for the game was written in Java using Swing API to construct the game's graphical user interface (GUI). The sources files for Fever Fighters can be accessed through the company's GitHub repository.

Level 1 of the game, where the player practices using the WASD controls of the game and collects a series a "good" and "bad" items to learn about the do's and don'ts of surviving a cold.

Level 2 of the game, where the player tests their knowledge from Level 1 by completing a maze they cannot see all at once by follwing only good items and avoiding bad ones.

Level 3 of the game, where the player is presented with a challenge to finally survive their cold. The player is running through an endless hallway and must move from side to side to collect 5000 points worth of good items while avoiding bad items, which make them lose one of their 3 lives.
